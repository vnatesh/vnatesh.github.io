Projects

A Network-wide Programming Model

For my master’s thesis, I have been working on a programming model, inspired by stream processing, that takes a high-level specification of a network program and compiles it into runnable code (in P4 or C) that can be launched on the programmable devices of network [2]. In this model, a network-wide program consists of a directed acyclic graph (DAG) where each node represents some high-level code snippet to be executed on packets arriving at that node and edges represent the order in which to execute the nodes. This thesis endeavors to make networks considerably easier to program..

Packet Stream-SQL

During the summer of 2018, I conducted an independent study to accelerate stream processing performance to terabit per second speeds using a virtual programmable switch. In this system, a packet contains a tuple payload with several named fields and corresponding values. Packets are continuously injected into a Mininet emulator where the virtual router filters packets based on a user query and forwards the results to a virtual host. I successfully implemented several SQL-like streaming primitives including filter (WHERE), project (SELECT), join (INNER JOIN), and aggregate operators (GROUPBY). To the left is a demo of a simple JOIN operation between two streams. Host 1 sends a stream of ad clicks while Host 2 sends a stream of ad impressions. The two streams are efficiently joined on the "adId" field at the virtual switch and the query result is sent to Host 3.

SELECT adId, impression_time, click_time
FROM impressions i
INNER JOIN clicks c ON i.adId = c.adId

p-adic numbers and the Hasse-Minkowski Theorem

Here is a paper I wrote in 2014 on p-adic numbers as part of an honors Number Theory course and the Distinguished Major Program. I delivered a lecture on this topic. Given a polynomial f ∈ Z[x1, x2, . . . , xn], determing the existence of integral roots is a standard topic covered in an introductory number theory course. In this paper, we will extend our considerations to roots in Q. Using p-adic numbers, the existence of rational solutions can be readily acertained under select conditions, especially for quadratic forms

RSA public key encryption

This paper is a survey of RSA encryption I wrote during my second year (2012). The RSA public key cryptosystem is a method of encryption which was made public in 1977. Named after its inventors, Ron Rivest, Adi Shamir, and Leonard Adleman, the method relies on the fact that large integers, on the order of hundreds of digits, require an extraordinary amount of time for a computer to factor. This paper explores the general procedure for RSA encryption, as well as a look into tests of primality

Publications

Risk Factors Associated with Progression to Blindness from Primary Open-Angle Glaucoma in an African-American Population.

This study investigates the risk factors associated with progression to blindness as a result of POAG in the African-American population. Statistical analysis was conducted per-subject for subject-specific measurements and per-eye for eye-specific measurements. For the subject-specific measurements, the comparison of characteristics between cases and matched controls was performed using the paired t-test for comparing means, Wilcoxon signed rank test for comparing medians, and McNemar’s test for comparing proportions. For eye-specific measurements, generalized estimating equations were used to account for correlations from matching and from paired eyes of a patient.28 To identify risk factors for blindness, univariate logistic regression models were first performed, followed by a multivariate logistic regression model that included risk factors with p < 0.10 from the univariate analysis. The multivariate logistic model went through backward model selection to reach the final model that only included risk factors at p < 0.05. In these logistic regression analyses, correlations from matching and from paired eyes of a subject were accounted for using generalized estimating equations. It was found that access to care, initial visual acuity worse than 20/40, and poor control of intraocular pressure were the major risk factors associated with blindness from POAG.

The SCHEIE Visual Field Grading System

No method of grading visual field (VF) defects has been widely accepted throughout the glaucoma community. The SCHEIE (Systematic Classification of Humphrey visual fields-Easy Interpretation and Evaluation) grading system for glaucomatous visual fields was created to convey qualitative and quantitative information regarding visual field defects in an objective, reproducible, and easily applicable manner for research purposes.
